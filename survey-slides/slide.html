Academic Signals: Validating Production Challenges

Recent academic work highlights three key trends that mirror real-world production issues.

1. Systematic Safety Threats

Formal benchmarks now confirm that Indirect Prompt Injection (IPI) is a persistent vulnerability across agent frameworks. The key finding is the prevalence of "quiet failures," which demand audit-quality trajectory traces to detect.

2. Cost as a Core Research Problem

Token efficiency is no longer just an engineering concern. Top-tier research now treats it as a first-class objective, documenting non-linear token growth (~7.5×) as a fundamental challenge in multi-agent systems that requires new cost-aware strategies.

3. The Need for a Balanced View on Strategy

A more pragmatic perspective is emerging, with studies showing that complex multi-agent patterns do not always outperform simpler, cheaper alternatives. This reinforces the need for empirical cost-benefit analysis for any deployed agent architecture.

Key Studies: InjecAgent (ACL 2024), S²-MAD (NAACL 2025), arXiv:2502.08788

Two Gaps & Requirements

Gap 1: Instrumentation Gap

Causal signal lost in high-volume system noise. SDKs are brittle, and closed components are inaccessible.

Gap 2: Semantic Gap

Boundary telemetry shows WHAT happened, not WHY (lacks intent and reasoning).

Vision: Two-Plane Architecture

Data Plane

  • Capture at stable boundaries (model, TLS, syscalls)
  • No in-app SDK required
  • Map to OTel GenAI spans
  • Privacy-first: Redaction, sampling

Cognitive Plane

  • "Agents observing agents"
  • Semantic evals, trajectory reconstruction
  • Policy actions: Quarantine, budget caps
Together: A closed loop for safety, cost, and control.
Key Metrics: Attack capture rate, $/successful task, runaway prevention, quiet failure detection.

Vision: Cognitive Plane (Algorithms & Outputs)

Algorithms

  • Semantic evaluation: Hallucination, loop, tool-misuse detection
  • Decision trajectory reconstruction (Watson-style surrogate observers)
  • Multi-layer causality graph (decisions → costs → actions → outcomes)
  • Cost policies: $/task, tokens/solve thresholds

Outputs & Governance Actions

  • Security: Alerts, isolation, policy enforcement, quarantine
  • Cost: Budgets, rate limiting, fallback policies, routing to cheaper agents
  • Auditability: Multi-agent causal chains, structured summaries

Privacy & Compliance

Redaction/masking, sampling (1-10% full / 90-99% metadata), scoped retention: 7d full / 90d aggregates / ∞ audit logs, OTel privacy attributes, MCP policy metadata, consent & transparency for compliance.
